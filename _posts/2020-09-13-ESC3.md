### Exercise 1
> Calculate the **electrical energy efficiency** of a reversible $H_2O \rightarrow H_2 + ½O_2$ electrochemical cell operating in discharge or fuel cell mode at a current density of $0.5\ [A/cm^2]$. Enthalpy of the reaction $\Delta H_r$ is 286 kJ/mol. Assume:
>- OCV of 1.0 V
>- perfectly linear current voltage characteristics with an ASR of $0.5 \Omega\ cm^2$ , for simplicity
>- 100% of the reactant is utilized in the system (no loss)

Solution:

$$\frac{1.0[V] - 0.5[A/cm^2]\cdot 0.5[\Omega/cm^2]}{\dfrac{286[kH/mol]}{2.96485[C/mol]}} = 50.6 \%$$

> Calculate the **cell electrical energy efficiency** for operating in charge or electrolysis mode at a current density of $0.5\ [A/cm^2]$. Same assumptions. How much heat $W/cm^2$ is produced or consumed by the cell?

Solution:

$$\frac{\dfrac{286[kH/mol]}{2.96485[C/mol]}}{1.0[V] - 0.5[A/cm^2]\cdot 0.5[\Omega/cm^2]} = 118.6\%$$

Heat Consumed:

$$\dfrac{286[kH/mol]}{2.96485[C/mol]} = 1.48\ [V]$$

$$1.0[V] - 0.5[A/cm^2]\cdot 0.5[\Omega/cm^2] = 1.25[V]$$

$$(1.48[V]-1.25[V])\cdot 0.5[A/cm^2] = 0.115\ [W/cm^2]$$

Electricity:(???)

$$1.25[V]\cdot 0.5[A/cm^2] = 0.625[W/cm^2]$$

### Exercise 2
> What is the storage media cost (or chemical cost) in kWh capacity for electrolytic $H_2O \rightarrow H_2 + ½O_2$ storage if the cost of purified, desalinated water is 1 $/m^3 ? (Use higher heating value or enthalpy of reaction.)

Solution:

$$\frac{1d}{m^3} \cdot \frac{cm^3}{1g} \cdot \frac{18g}{mol} \cdot \frac{mol}{286kJ} = 0.002\ dollar/kWhr$$


>It will be used as a reversible battery like system with the hydrogen stored in a 700-bar pressure vessel that costs 27 $/L. Assuming ideal gas, what is this container cost in $/ kWh_capacity?

$$\frac{27}{L} \cdot \frac{25\degree C \cdot 8.314 J/molK}{700\ bar} \cdot \frac{mol}{286kJ} = 12\ dollar/kWhr$$
